Why visit India

India offers a uniquely diverse and enriching travel experience. The country spans snow-capped Himalayas, deserts, forests, plains, islands, and a long coastline—so you can plan anything from nature and adventure to slow, scenic getaways.

It’s also home to iconic monuments and architectural wonders such as the Taj Mahal (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), the Red Fort in Delhi, Hampi’s ancient ruins, the Brihadeeswara Temple, and the Mysore Palace—each reflecting centuries of cultural influence.

Top itineraries

Royal Beauty

Jaipur

Jaipur, the Pink City, blends royal history with vibrant markets and stunning architecture. Its famous pink buildings trace back to 1876, creating a distinctive and memorable cityscape.

Udaipur

Udaipur is known for lakeside views and elegant palaces. Highlights include the City Palace complex and Jag Mandir on Lake Pichola, plus gardens and panoramic viewpoints.

Jodhpur

Jodhpur’s blue-painted lanes and grand forts make it a standout in Rajasthan. Don’t miss Jaswant Thada, Umaid Bhawan Palace, and Mandore Gardens.

Mysore

Mysore is famous for Dasara celebrations and a relaxed, green atmosphere. The illuminated Mysore Palace is a major highlight for visitors.

Hyderabad

Explore Charminar, Golconda Fort, Chowmahalla Palace, the Salar Jung Museum, and the Qutb Shahi Tombs for a deep look into the city’s history and architecture.

Lucknow

The City of Nawabs is known for Awadhi culture and grand architecture. See Bara Imambara, Chota Imambara, and the iconic Rumi Darwaza.

Lakes and Falls

Chilika Lake: Odisha

Chilika is a large brackish-water lagoon known for scenic views and biodiversity. In winter, migratory birds arrive from many regions.

Wular Lake: Jammu & Kashmir

Wular is one of Asia’s largest freshwater lakes, surrounded by Himalayan scenery. Its size changes with the seasons, creating an ever-shifting landscape.

Gurudongmar Lake: Sikkim

Gurudongmar Lake is a high-altitude, sacred lake near the Tibetan border, known for pristine views and dramatic surroundings.

Adventure Sports

Rishikesh: White-Water Rafting

Rishikesh, nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas, is India’s premier destination for white-water rafting on the Ganges River. The river offers thrilling rapids ranging from Grade I (calm) to Grade V (challenging), suitable for both beginners and experts. Most expeditions run between September and November, when water levels are ideal. Professional guides, safety gear, and training sessions ensure a safe yet adrenaline-pumping experience. Beyond rafting, you can try bungee jumping, giant swings, and camping by the riverside.

Manali: Trekking & Paragliding

Manali in Himachal Pradesh is a hub for Himalayan adventures. Popular treks include the Solang Valley (easy-moderate, 3-4 hours) and the challenging Beas Kund trek (7-8 hours) that leads to a high-altitude alpine lake. For aerial thrills, paragliding at Solang Valley offers stunning views of snow-capped peaks. Winter transforms the region into a ski paradise, with slopes suitable for all skill levels. The best time to visit is April to June (summer) or December to February (winter sports).

Andaman Islands: Scuba Diving

The Andamans are home to some of India’s best scuba diving spots, with crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and marine life like sharks, rays, and sea turtles. Popular sites include Havelock Island’s Elephant Beach and Neil Island’s Bharatpur. Courses range from beginner (PADI Open Water) to advanced dives. The diving season runs from October to May, avoiding monsoon rough seas. Most operators provide full equipment, boat rides, and certified instructors for a safe underwater experience.

Ladakh: Motorcycling

Ladakh’s high-altitude desert terrain makes it a legend for motorcycling. The Leh-Manali Highway (500 km) is one of Asia’s most thrilling rides, with passes like Rohtang (13,050 ft) and Khardung La (18,380 ft). Best ridden between June and September when roads are snow-free. Riders need acclimatization to avoid AMS (Acute Mountain Sickness). Along the route, you’ll find monasteries, crystal-blue lakes like Pangong Lake, and opportunities for camping under star-filled skies.
